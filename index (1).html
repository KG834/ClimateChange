Why should we care about the ocean?

Why should we care about the ocean? Well, one reason is that it regulates the climate for us humans. It also provides us with a food source and transportation. The ocean regulates climate by distributing heat around the globe and sucking in Co2. It provides us with fish, a carbon efficient food source, which millions of people depend on every day. That means if you're killing the ocean, you're also starving the same millions of people who depend on fish as a food source. The Ocean is in Trouble

A problem currently facing the ocean is climate change. Climate change is also in turn causing ocean acidification and sea level rise. Climate change is being caused by humans burning fossil fuels which releases Co2 into the air. This is causing the oceans pH levels to have risen by 0.1 pH in a matter of years. This is causing coral reefs, a habitat for thousands of species, to die because of the new ph levels. Climate change is also causing the sea level to rise, and this could flood large coastal cities like San Francisco and Miami which would in turn destroy millions of homes. What NOAA Does to Help

NOAA stands for National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, and it researches the ocean and the atmosphere and protects them. Watersheds are an area of land where drains all the streams and rainfall drain to a common outlet such as the outflow of a reservoir, mouth of a bay, or any point along a stream. There are big watersheds like the Mississippi River Watershed, or a small watershed like a tiny stream that drains into a small pond. Our local watershed is the San Leandro Creek Watershed. Our National Marine Sanctuaries are basically underwater national parks that NOAA manages. There are 15 total National Marine Sanctuaries, 2 Marine National Monuments, and 2 proposed National Marine Sanctuaries. Our local National Marine Sanctuary is the Greater Farallones.
